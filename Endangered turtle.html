TURTLES AS AN ENDANGERED SPECIES


Sea turtles are among the creatures that have been cruising through the ocean for a million years. In total, there are 7 different types
of sea turtles that have existed. However, in the past century, the number of sea turtle in the world's oceans is getting smaller and smaller
day by day, taking the death toll of sea turtles in an increasement. All of this is due to mankind's selfish activities which has destructed
the survival scale of these ancient mariners.


        

1. Commercial fishing
•Thousands of sea turtles become entangled in fishing nets every year.
They mostly get injured during the struggle to free themselves from
the fishing net.




        

2. Pollution
• When we pollute the sea with plastic bags, the ocean becomes a dangerous
habitat for turtles. This is because turtles mostly eat jellyfish which looks
like plastic bags. When they accidentally consume plastic bags, they can choke and die.




        

3. Oil spillage
• Oil spills from chemicals, fertilizers and petroleum pollutes the ocean.
The toxins released from these pollutants disrupts the food chain of the turtles.




        

4. Poaching
• The high human demand of turtle meat, eggs, skin and shells has led to turtle poaching.
For your information, researches have estimated that more than 50 thousand marine turtles
are killed in Southeast Asia and the South Pacific for this purpose every year.




        

5. Beach activities
• For example, human's activities at night such as flashing lights prevents female sea turtles from emerging
on the beach for nesting and cause them to return to the ocean.
